The gap

Most couples don't need more information.
They need an understanding.

When attachment needs feel threatened, the nervous system reacts, often in ways that make things worse. One partner reaches, the other withdraws.

It's not a failure of love.
It's the absence of support.

(re)connect lives in that space.

Couples therapy is powerful, but it's weekly, costly, and often begins too late. Most couples who need support never get it. And those who do are largely on their own between sessions.

Most couples wait too long. Not because they don't care, but because nothing existed in between. By the time they look for help, something's already broken.

What makes it different

Most relationship tools
only hear one side.

Most apps ask one person how the relationship is going. That person answers honestly, from their own experience. And it's incomplete.

(re)connect was built around a different idea. Each partner engages independently, privately, without knowing what the other said.

Two sides of the story.
One truth of the relationship.

That synthesis is the product. It gets sharper the longer you use it, learning the patterns that repeat, the moments that matter. Not a generic algorithm. Something that knows you.

How it works

A few minutes a day.
A different relationship over time.

01
Each of you shares your experience, privately.
Every day, each partner takes a few minutes to reflect on the relationship independently. Closeness. Support. Conflict. Appreciation. Trust. Your answers are yours alone, not compared, not shared. This is your (re)turn: a daily return to yourself, and toward your partner.
02
The platform reads both sides.
When both partners complete their (re)turn, (re)connect synthesizes what neither could see alone. Not "you said this, they said that." The shared need beneath the conflict, named without blaming either of you.
03
Over time, it notices what you can't.
The system watches across weeks and months, seeing patterns before you do, knowing when stress is showing up relationally. It delivers what this specific relationship needs, and gets more precise the longer you use it.
